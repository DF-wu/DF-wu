I think about what happens **after** you ship. Will the person who inherits this codebase six months later understand what I was thinking? Will the ops team trust it at 3am? That's the bar I hold myself to.

I write Java, Python, Go, and TypeScript. I run Docker and Linux in production. I've built things with Spring, Gin, MySQL, MongoDB. But the technology matters less than the outcome: a system that's stable, maintainable, and doesn't require heroics to operate.

I'm active in Taiwan's civic tech community through [g0v.social](https://g0v.social/@df), and I run a home-lab where the infrastructure is my own code. If my services break, I'm the one getting paged.

### Things I've built

**[HideReplier](https://github.com/DF-wu/HideReplier)** — My most complete project. Java, full-stack. I owned the entire lifecycle: backend logic, workflow design, deployment. This is where I learned what "end-to-end delivery" actually means when there's nobody else to hand things off to.

**[CCTS](https://github.com/DF-wu/CCTS)** — An architecture experiment I keep coming back to. I use it to test whether my design decisions hold up under iteration. Some patterns survive, some don't. The ones that don't teach me more.

**[myServices](https://github.com/DF-wu/myServices)** — Docker-based service composition that runs my home-lab. This isn't a portfolio piece. It's running right now. When I say "deployment-oriented," I mean I live with the consequences.

**[iDRACFanSpeedControl](https://github.com/DF-wu/iDRACFanSpeedControl)** — A small shell script that automates server thermal management. I wrote it once, deployed it, and haven't touched it since. My server room is quiet now. That's what good automation looks like.

**[BehaviorMonitor](https://github.com/DF-wu/BehaviorMonitor)** — AI-assisted prototyping in TypeScript. I wanted to see if AI could be a practical engineering tool, not just a demo. The answer is yes — if you treat it seriously.

### If you're hiring

I'm best on teams that value:
- **Stability over speed-at-all-costs.** I ship things that don't wake people up at night.
- **Maintainability from day one.** I design for the engineer who comes after me.
- **Low entropy.** I reduce architecture complexity before it compounds.

My first month, I'll map your architecture and find where the team loses time. Second month, I'll start making targeted, low-risk improvements. By the third month, the system is more stable than when I arrived and the team is moving a little faster.

I don't bring drama. I bring working systems.
